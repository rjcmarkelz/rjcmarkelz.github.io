We planned a back to back ultra-runs from our hometown of Mount Shasta, CA up into the Klamath range. For my part, I wanted to balance ease of running (established trail as much as possible), with running through areas with old burns and recent burns. This route has a balance of all of these with additional road access for our crew. The light blue is the first day and the dark blue is the second day. The orange outlines are fire scars from the last 100 years (Data Source, CALFIRE).

DAY 1 - Thursday August 3 - Mount Shasta to Scott Mountain Campground

39 miles - +6335/-4501 (feet gain/loss) Trails: Sission Trail to PCT Northbound Midway Stop: Parks Creek PCT Trailhead Directions from Mount Shasta to Scott Mountain Campground Scott Mountain Campground Weather Forecast

DAY 2 - Friday August 4 - Scott Mountain to Etna Summit

39 miles - (+7826/-7289) Trail:PCT Northbound Midway Stop: Scott Mountain to Hidden Lake Trail - 30 minute drive Directions from Hidden Lake to Etna Summit - 54 minute drive Etna Summit Forecast

In the next few posts in this series I will talk about the fire history of each of the many fire scars we ran by or directly through and what story they have to tell about the pyroscape in my backyard.
